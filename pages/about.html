About this project

Why this course exists

This course was built as a free, open resource for high school and tertiary students navigating AI tools in their coursework. Most existing guidance falls into one of two camps: total bans on AI, or guides on dodging plagiarism checkers. Neither actually helps a student figure out how to use these tools well.

This site is the third option — a practical guide to using AI as a genuine learning aid, understanding why academic integrity matters beyond punishment, and knowing how to disclose and cite AI assistance properly.

Scope

This course does not cover, and will not add, content about evading AI-detection software or disguising AI-generated text. That's a different goal from the one this site is built for.

Who it's for

High school students preparing for tertiary study, and first-year university or college students adjusting to academic integrity expectations that are often stricter — and more clearly enforced — than what they experienced in school.

Built with

Plain HTML, plain CSS, and vanilla JavaScript — no build step, no frameworks, hosted on GitHub Pages. The source is open if you want to adapt it for your own school or course.
