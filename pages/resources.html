Resources

Official guidance and tools worth bookmarking. Always defer to your own institution's policy where it differs from general guidance below.

Style guides

Official referencing style sites

APA Style, MLA Style Center, and Cite Them Right (Harvard) all publish current guidance on citing AI tools, updated as conventions evolve.

Search "[your style] cite AI" plus the current year for the latest version.

Institutional policy

Your own institution's AI policy

Most universities and colleges publish an AI use policy on their academic integrity or library page. This always overrides general advice — check it first.

Search "[your institution name] AI policy academic integrity."

Study skills

Active recall & spaced repetition

These evidence-based study techniques pair well with AI as a quiz partner (see Module 5). Worth understanding why they work, not just that they do.

Search "active recall spaced repetition study technique."

Plagiarism basics

What counts as plagiarism

Most institutional libraries publish a plain-language plagiarism guide covering paraphrasing, common knowledge, and self-plagiarism — useful background beyond AI specifically.

Search "[your institution] library plagiarism guide."

If you're unsure

The fastest way to resolve uncertainty about a specific assignment is to ask your teacher or lecturer directly. "Can I use AI for this, and if so, how should I cite it?" is a completely reasonable question — asking it upfront is always better than guessing.
