Reference correctly

Citing AI assistance

Always check your institution's specific requirement first — these are general templates based on each style's official guidance, current as of 2025–2026. Verify against your style guide's official site if precision matters for grading.

Before you cite anything

Confirm AI use is permitted for this specific task. A correct citation doesn't make disallowed AI use acceptable.

Pick your referencing style

All three styles, side by side

APA (7th edition)

APA treats AI output as a non-retrievable source — similar to citing a personal communication, but with a reference list entry since the content is reproducible by you.

In-text:

(OpenAI, 2026)

Reference list:

OpenAI. (2026). ChatGPT (Mar 2026 version) [Large language model]. https://chat.openai.com

Harvard

Harvard style (used widely in South African and UK institutions) typically cites the AI tool as the "author" with the year and access details.

In-text:

(Claude, Anthropic 2026)

Reference list:

Anthropic (2026) Claude [Large language model]. Available at: https://claude.ai (Accessed: 28 June 2026).

MLA (9th edition)

MLA recommends describing the prompt used and treating the AI tool as a container/publisher in the works-cited entry.

Works cited:

"Describe the causes of the French Revolution" prompt. ChatGPT, 24 Mar. version, OpenAI, 28 June 2026, chat.openai.com.

Beyond the reference list

Many courses now also ask for a short methodology note describing how you used AI — e.g. "used to check grammar in the final draft" or "used to generate practice questions during revision." Check your assignment brief for this requirement; it's becoming more common than a plain citation.
